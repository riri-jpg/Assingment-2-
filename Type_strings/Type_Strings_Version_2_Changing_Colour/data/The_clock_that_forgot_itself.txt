The Clock That Forgot Itself

There was a clock on Pemberton Street
that lost its hands one Tuesday in June.
It stood in the window of a pawnshop
between a trombone and a silver spoon.
Nobody came to buy the broken thing.
Nobody wound it. Nobody cared.
But every evening around half past nothing
it chimed for a world that was no longer there.
The pigeons learned to roost on its face.
The rain wore grooves in its wooden side.
It counted the years in rust and silence,
measuring time by how much had died.
And when they tore the pawnshop down
and carted the rubble away in trucks,
the clock was the last thing standing there,
still ticking through the debris and the dust.
Some clocks, you see, don't need their hands.
Some clocks just need a reason to go.
And that old clock on Pemberton Street
kept the only time it would ever know.BLAST
